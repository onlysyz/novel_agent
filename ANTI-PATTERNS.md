# Anti-Patterns for Novel Writing

Core Rule: **Show, don't tell**. Trust the image, the gesture, the silence. Cut explanations after emotional beats.

---

## Structural Anti-Patterns

### 1. The Over-Explain
Don't restate what the scene already showed. Cut paragraphs that analyze a gesture or emotion after it occurs.

### 2. Triadic Listing
Avoid "X. Y. Z." or "A and B and C." Combinations of two items are often stronger than three.

### 3. Negative-Assertion Repetition
Limit "He did not..." constructions. Maximum 1 per chapter. Replace with active alternatives.

### 4. Cataloging-by-Thinking
Real interiority is messy. Don't list thoughts as topics. Use fragments, action, or dialogue instead.

### 5. The Simile Crutch
Limit "the way X did Y" to 2 per chapter. Prefer direct metaphor over simile.

### 6. Section Break Overuse
Maximum 2 breaks per chapter. Write continuous prose instead of vignettes.

### 7. Uniform Paragraph Length
Vary deliberately: include 1-2 sentence paragraphs for impact, 6+ for building tension.

### 8. Predictable Emotional Arcs
Include one surprising moment per chapter: a character says the wrong thing.

### 9. Repetitive Endings
No two chapters should end with the same structural move.

### 10. Rhetorical Dialogue Formulas
Vary sentence structures. Characters using the same formula sound identical.

### 11. Over-Polished Dialogue
Include false starts, interruptions, and imperfect lines. Characters shouldn't speak in epigrams.

### 12. Scene-Summary Imbalance
70%+ of each chapter should be in-scene. Save summary for time compression only.

---

## AI-Specific Patterns to Avoid

### Prose Patterns
- Observations in threes
- On-schedule emotional beats
- Cataloging description (listing multiple items in sequence)
- Internal monologue explaining what the scene already showed
- Monotonous paragraph lengths

### Dialogue Patterns
- Characters who never stumble or say slightly wrong things
- Characters who always say the right thing strategically
- Everyone has clever comebacks
- No awkward silences

### Structure Patterns
- Every scene advances the plot
- No "quiet" chapters
- Excessive setup before payoff
- Foreshadowing that's too obvious

---

## Key Principles

1. **Variation defeats detection** — Predictable patterns signal AI generation
2. **Real prose is messier** — Editing creates polish; first drafts should feel alive
3. **Trust the reader** — Infer meaning rather than stating it
4. **Distinct characters need distinct voices** — Speech patterns, vocabulary, rhythm
5. **Earned tension** — Tension through scene work, not narrator assertions
